Soil Health Management

  • Local Soil Testing: Use affordable or simple DIY soil tests.
    • Place 2 tablespoons of soil in a bowl and add ½ cup vinegar. If the mixture fizzes, you have alkaline soil.
    • Place 2 tablespoons of soil in a bowl and moisten it with distilled water. Add ½ cup baking soda. If the mixture fizzes, you have acidic soil.
  • Crop Rotation: Make sure to rotate what crops are in what fields as they all produce different minerals for the soil, to avoid over-saturation of one. Crops in each field need to be rotated.
  • Cover Cropping: If possible, use readily available cover crops like mung beans or cowpeas to protect and enrich the soil.
  • Organic Matter: Utilize local organic waste (e.g., rice husks, animal manure) to improve soil fertility.

Water Management

  • Rainwater Harvesting: Install basic rainwater collection systems using available materials.
  • Soil Moisture Retention: Apply mulch from local materials such as straw or dried parts of previously grown crops.

Intgrated Pest Management

  • Regular Monitoring: Conduct simple, regular checks for pest activity.
  • Biological Control: Encourage natural predators like birds or beneficial insects by maintaining habitats.

Sustainable Crop Production

  • Resilient Varieties: Choose local crop varieties that are naturally resistant to pests and diseases. (Rice)
  • Low-cost Techniques: Adopt simple precision farming practices, such as proper spacing and timing of planting as well as watering timings.

Waste Management

  • Recycling and Composting: Use simple composting methods for organic waste this will also allow for compost to later be reused to enrich the soil.
  • Waste Plan: Develop a basic waste management plan utilizing community resource.
